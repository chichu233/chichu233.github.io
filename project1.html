Dynamic Masked Transformer for Human-Object Interaction

January 2023 - Present Computer Vision, Transformer, Deep Learning

Abstract

This project introduces a novel transformer-based architecture for detecting human-object interactions (HOI) with dynamic attention masking. Traditional HOI detection methods often struggle with complex scenes containing multiple humans and objects. Our approach dynamically adjusts the attention mechanism based on spatial relationships and semantic relevance between human and object instances.

The key innovation is the Dynamic Masking Module (DMM) that learns to focus on relevant human-object pairs while suppressing irrelevant ones. This significantly reduces computational overhead and improves accuracy. We evaluate our method on two benchmark datasets (HICO-DET and V-COCO), achieving state-of-the-art performance with 5-7% improvement in mean average precision compared to previous approaches.

The model also demonstrates superior generalization capabilities in crowded scenes and shows promising results in real-time applications. This work has potential applications in surveillance, human-robot interaction, and augmented reality systems.

Project Details

  • Technologies: PyTorch, Transformer Architecture, Computer Vision
  • Team: Individual project with advisor consultation
  • Dataset: HICO-DET (38,118 images), V-COCO (10,346 images)
  • Performance: 32.5 mAP on HICO-DET, 58.7 mAP on V-COCO
  • Innovation: Dynamic attention masking reduces computation by 40%
  • Status: Ongoing - preparing for journal submission
